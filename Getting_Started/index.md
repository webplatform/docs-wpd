<h1><span class="mw-headline" id="Start_contributing_content">Start contributing content</span></h1>
<p>Anyone can contribute to the WebPlatform.org wiki. <b>First</b>, read the <a href="/wiki/WPD:Contributors_Guide" title="WPD:Contributors Guide">Contributor's Guide</a> to learn how to set up your account, find information about how we work, and get the details about editing content. Then, come back here and dig in!
</p><p>This page provides lists of pages that need your help; these are broken out by task. Scroll down the page to find a task you would like to perform. In the table for each task, click the link to a page that needs the work. 
</p><p><b>As you work, do the following:</b>
</p>
<ol><li> Check out the page you are working on so that other editors do not attempt to work on the same page.
<dl><dd> Edit the page, check the box, <b>Checked out (this article is currently being worked on)</b>.</dd>
<dd> Save the page, in the Summary for the edit, enter "Checking out."</dd>
<dd> Then reopen it and resume working.</dd>
<dd> Do not forget to uncheck the box when you are done editing the page, and in the Summary, enter "Checking in."</dd></dl></li>
<li> If you have any questions, <a href="/wiki/WPD:Editors_Guide/step_2_communicate_with_the_online_community" title="WPD:Editors Guide/step 2 communicate with the online community"> just ask the community on IRC or e-mail</a>.
<dl><dd> </dd></dl></li>
<li> Refer to <a href="/wiki/WPD:Editors_Guide/step_5_update_existing_content" title="WPD:Editors Guide/step 5 update existing content"> Step 5 of the Editor's Guide</a> for information about editing pages.
<dl><dd></dd></dl></li>
<li> As you finish a chunk of work, clear the checkbox for the work issue (i.e. uncheck "Needs Summary" after you fill in the summary).</li></ol>
<p>The tasks are listed by degree of experience and knowledge required. As you move from the basic tasks to more advanced tasks, you will gain greater expertise -- both in using the wiki and in the subject matter. Choose from the following types of tasks:

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fix_content_that_is_not_semantic">Fix content that is not semantic</span></h2>
<p>(0 results)
</p><p>WebPlatform uses Semantic Media Wiki and Semantic Forms to organize information in a structured way. Sometimes, however, the information is represented on a page in an unstructured field. Often it is as simple as moving content from one text box to another. However, it may be that the extant forms do not provide a way to represent the information semantically, in which case the resolution first requires a new form/template to be added to the wiki. For that case, you would leave the "Data Not Semantic" flag checked and <a href="/wiki/WPD:Filing_Bugs" title="WPD:Filing Bugs">file a bug</a> to request the new form/template.
</p><p>For example, in the page for the <a href="/wiki/apis/webrtc/MediaStreamTrack/readyState" title="apis/webrtc/MediaStreamTrack/readyState">MediaStreamTrack.readyState</a> property, the return value describes three possible enum values with raw text. A SMW form would more properly identify the values as an enum with a table of name, numerator, and description information for each value.
</p><p>This type of issue is difficult to spot, but it is pervasive throughout the wiki. Basically, any time you see structured information - like an enum - represented in an unstructured way, you should check the <b>Data Not Semantic</b> flag for the page where you find that information.
</p><p>See also the <a href="/wiki/WPD:Implementation_Patterns" title="WPD:Implementation Patterns">Implementation Patterns page</a> to learn more about how we use forms and templates to organize and present information.<br /><br />
The following criteria are applied:
</p>
<ul><li> High-level issues: Data Not Semantic</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Review_content">Review content</span></h2>
<p>The great thing about a wiki is that it's always under review. However, when an article is marked with the <b>Needs Review</b> flag, the author has specifically requested help from the community in verifying the content. Reviewing an article requires a working knowledge of the subject, and often requires the testing of code samples. When you review an article, always check it against the most recent specifications. See <a href="/wiki/WPD:Editors_Guide/step_4_review_existing_content" title="WPD:Editors Guide/step 4 review existing content">Review existing content</a> for reviewing guidelines.
</p><p><br />

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_involved_tasks">Other involved tasks</span></h2>
<p>Herein are some other ideas for contributing to the docs.
</p>
<ul><li> <b>Add screenshots or supporting information</b> <br />Many articles have broken screenshot links, or include notes that a screenshot is needed. See the steps to <a href="/wiki/WPD:Editors_Guide/step_7_prepare_and_upload_assets_for_articles" title="WPD:Editors Guide/step 7 prepare and upload assets for articles"> Prepare and upload screenshots</a>, or provide examples and supporting information to illustrate techniques.</li>
<li> <b>Split articles</b>  <br />Create pages that convey a single, specific concept by splitting up long pages that contain a wide variety of different content.</li>
<li> <b>Write new articles</b> <br />If you'd like to author new pages,  see the suggestion board to find topics to write. If you are not sure if your topic idea is needed, <a href="/wiki/WPD:Editors_Guide/step_2_communicate_with_the_online_community" title="WPD:Editors Guide/step 2 communicate with the online community"> send a message to the team</a>. If your topic suggestion is approved and you do not have time to write the entire article, it is still worth creating a new page with the beginnings of a new article on it. (This is called a stub page). To get more information about creating new pages, see <a href="/wiki/WPD:Editors_Guide/step_6_author_or_upload_new_content" title="WPD:Editors Guide/step 6 author or upload new content"> Step 6 of the Editor's Guide</a>.</li>
<li> <b>Suggest new topics to write</b>  <br />If you have a good idea for an article that could be included but don't have the time or skills to write it yourself, it is still worth putting the idea forward. <a href="/wiki/WPD:Editors_Guide/step_2_communicate_with_the_online_community" title="WPD:Editors Guide/step 2 communicate with the online community">Send a message</a> that outlines the topic and scope of the article.</li>
<li> <b>Improve internal documentation</b> <br />If you research the <a href="/wiki/WPD:Editors_Guide" title="WPD:Editors Guide" class="mw-redirect">Editor's Guide</a> and you cannot find out how to do something, <a href="/wiki/WPD:Editors_Guide/step_2_communicate_with_the_online_community" title="WPD:Editors Guide/step 2 communicate with the online community">propose an update to the Editor's Guide</a> to make the process easier for other contributors.</li>
